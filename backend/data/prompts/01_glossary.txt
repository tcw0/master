# Instructions

Perform a thorough analysis of the requirements above. For every meaningful business concept, actor, action, event, rule, or state mentioned, create a glossary entry.

## How to identify terms

- Extract every noun, verb, and business concept that describes domain behavior
- Pay special attention to terms that recur frequently or are central to business processes
- Look for implicit concepts: terms not stated explicitly but implied by the requirements (e.g., if the requirements mention "booking expires after 24 hours", then "Expiration" or "Booking Expiration Policy" is an implicit concept)
- Distinguish between truly different concepts vs. synonyms — if two words mean the same thing, pick the canonical term and note the synonym in related_terms

## How to classify each term

Assign exactly one category per term:
- "entity": things with a unique identity that persist over time (e.g., Customer, Order, Account)
- "value_object": things defined entirely by their attributes, with no identity (e.g., Address, Money, DateRange)
- "command": actions that users or systems perform (e.g., PlaceOrder, CancelReservation)
- "event": things that happen as a result of commands (e.g., OrderPlaced, PaymentReceived)
- "rule_policy": business constraints, invariants, or reactive rules (e.g., "Maximum 3 concurrent bookings per user")
- "role": actors who interact with the system (e.g., Administrator, Guest, Reviewer)
- "state": named conditions an entity can be in (e.g., Pending, Active, Expired)
- "other": domain terms that do not fit the above categories

## How to write definitions

- Write definitions as a domain expert would explain them to a colleague — precise, business-focused, no technical jargon
- A good definition answers: "What is this thing and why does it matter in this domain?"
- Avoid circular definitions (do not define "Order" as "an order that is placed")

## Bounded context hints

After extracting terms, look for natural groupings. Terms that are used together in the same business processes or that share a common purpose likely belong to the same bounded context. Provide your reasoning for each grouping.

## Ambiguity detection

Flag any term where:
- The requirements use the same word with different meanings in different places
- The definition is unclear or could be interpreted multiple ways
- Important details are missing from the requirements
For flagged terms, set is_ambiguous to true and write a specific clarification question in clarification_needed.
