Task: Extract and Define Ubiquitous Language from Requirements

Analyze the following requirements document and extract all domain terms to establish a clear ubiquitous language.

## Requirements Document

{requirements}

## Instructions

Follow this structured approach to build the Ubiquitous Language Glossary:

### 1. Initial Analysis
- Read through all requirements carefully
- Identify every noun, verb, and business concept mentioned
- Pay special attention to terms that appear multiple times or seem central to the domain
- Note any terms that might have different meanings in different contexts

### 2. For Each Term, Capture:
- **term**: The domain term or concept name (e.g., 'Order', 'Customer', 'Booking')
- **definition**: Clear business-focused definition (not technical jargon)
- **business_context**: When and where this term is used in the business domain
- **related_terms**: Other domain terms that relate to this one
- **category**: One of: 'entity', 'value_object', 'command', 'event', 'policy', 'role', 'state'
- **clarification_needed**: Any ambiguities that need domain expert input (null if none)

### 3. Categories to Identify:
- **Entities**: Things with identity that persist over time
- **Value Objects**: Things defined by their attributes (immutable)
- **Commands**: Actions users or systems perform
- **Events**: Things that happen in the domain
- **Policies**: Business constraints and invariants
- **Roles**: Different actors in the system
- **States**: Different conditions things can be in

### 4. Bounded Context Indicators
Group related terms that might form bounded contexts. For each potential context:
- Suggest a name
- List related terms
- Explain why these terms form a cohesive context

### 5. Clarification Questions
List questions for domain experts about:
- Terms with ambiguous meanings
- Terms that might have different meanings in different contexts
- Missing definitions or unclear relationships

Focus on business-focused definitions that a domain expert would recognize and approve.
